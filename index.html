About me

I am a quantitative analyst on the systematic futures team at D.E. Shaw & Co..

I completed my Ph.D. at Princeton University, advised by Prof. Elad Hazan. I was generously awarded with the Gordon Wu Fellowship. My research focused on online learning and its intersection with optimization and nonstochastic control theory. During this time, I also worked as a student researcher on memory efficient optimization algorithms for the algorithmic efficiency team with Vlad Feinberg at Google DeepMind.

Previously, I graduated Summa Cum Laude and Phi Beta Kappa from The University of Chicago with a Bachelor of Science degree in Mathematics with honors.

I was born and raised in Shanghai, China. I moved to Pebble Beach, CA to attend Stevenson School at the age of 15. Those four years have been the most precious time in my life.
